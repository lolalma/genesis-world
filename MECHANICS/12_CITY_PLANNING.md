# City Planning and Urban Systems Documentation

## City Layout
- Define the overall layout of the city including road networks, public spaces, and residential areas.
- Emphasize accessibility and connectivity between different city sections.

## Districts
- Outline the different districts within the city:
  - **Residential District**: Housing units, parks, schools, and community centers.
  - **Commercial District**: Retail spaces, offices, and services.
  - **Industrial District**: Factories, warehouses, and distribution centers.
  - **Recreational District**: Parks, sports facilities, and entertainment zones.

## Zoning
- Describe the zoning regulations:
  - **Residential Zoning**: Types of residential buildings allowed and their densities.
  - **Commercial Zoning**: Regulations for commercial establishments, including restrictions on types.
  - **Industrial Zoning**: Requirements for industrial operations and their impact on surrounding areas.

## Infrastructure
- Discuss the essential infrastructure:
  - **Transportation**: Roads, public transit systems, bike lanes, and pedestrian pathways.
  - **Utilities**: Water supply, sewage, electricity, and internet.
  - **Waste Management**: Systems for effective waste collection and recycling.

## City Development
- Highlight strategies for sustainable development:
  - **Smart Growth**: Focus on efficient land use and minimizing urban sprawl.
  - **Community Involvement**: Engage residents in the planning and development process.
  - **Green Spaces**: Incorporate parks and green areas in development plans.

## Urban Design
- Principles of urban design to consider:
  - **Human Scale**: Design that prioritizes pedestrians and social interactions.
  - **Aesthetics**: Incorporate art and architecture that reflects the community's identity.
  - **Functionality**: Ensure that spaces are practical and meet the needs of residents.

This document serves as a guide to understanding the complexities of city planning and urban systems within the Genesis World project.
