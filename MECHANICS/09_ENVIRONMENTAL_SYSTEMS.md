# Environmental Systems Documentation

## Biomes
- **Forest:** Dense areas dominated by trees. Can vary from tropical rainforests to temperate deciduous forests.
- **Desert:** Characterized by low precipitation, extreme temperatures, and sparse vegetation.
- **Grassland:** Open and spacious areas, dominated by grasses, often found in regions with moderate rainfall.
- **Tundra:** Cold, treeless regions typically found in high latitudes or altitudes.
- **Wetlands:** Areas where water covers the soil or is present near the surface for part of the year, supporting diverse ecosystems.

## Climate Systems
- **Tropical Climate:** Warm temperatures year-round with distinct wet and dry seasons.
- **Temperate Climate:** Moderate temperatures with four distinct seasons.
- **Polar Climate:** Very cold temperatures and minimal precipitation, typically found near the poles.
- **Arid Climate:** Characterized by very low rainfall, leading to dry conditions.

## Natural Disasters
- **Earthquakes:** Sudden shaking of the ground, can cause widespread damage.
- **Volcanic Eruptions:** Explosive eruptions can lead to lava flows, ash clouds, and pyroclastic flows.
- **Tsunamis:** Large ocean waves caused by underwater earthquakes or volcanic eruptions.
- **Hurricanes:** Powerful storms with strong winds, heavy rain, and potential for flooding.

## Seasons
- **Spring:** Characterized by warming temperatures, flowering plants, and increased rainfall.
- **Summer:** Hot temperatures, longer days, and typically dry conditions in some regions.
- **Autumn (Fall):** Cooling temperatures, shedding of leaves, and harvest time for crops.
- **Winter:** Cold temperatures, potential for snow and ice, and shorter days.

## Weather
- **Sunny:** Clear skies and warm temperatures. Ideal for outdoor activities.
- **Cloudy:** Overcast skies with a potential for rain.
- **Rain:** Precipitation that can vary from light drizzles to heavy downpours.
- **Snow:** Frozen precipitation that covers the ground in winter.
- **Wind:** Air movement that can vary in intensity and direction.

## Environmental Hazards
- **Pollution:** Contamination of air, water, and soil, affecting health and ecosystems.
- **Deforestation:** The removal of trees, leading to loss of biodiversity and climate change.
- **Climate Change:** Long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns due to human activity.
- **Invasive Species:** Non-native species that can disrupt local ecosystems and outcompete native species.
