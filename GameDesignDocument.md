# Game Design Document for Genesis World

## Introduction
This document serves as a comprehensive blueprint for the Genesis World game, outlining the various mechanics that will shape the player experience. 

### Gameplay Mechanics

#### 1. Agent Behavior
- Agents will exhibit various behaviors based on their personality traits, environmental factors, and learned experiences.
- Use of AI to simulate realistic interactions between agents, including collaboration and conflict.

#### 2. Civilization Progression
- Civilizations will progress through defined eras, each unlocking new technologies and cultural traits.
- Players can influence progression through choices and achievements.

#### 3. Building Systems
- A dynamic building system allowing players to construct various structures with specific functionalities.
- Buildings will have upgrades and maintenance requirements.

#### 4. Genetics
- Incorporation of genetic traits that influence the abilities and growth of agents.
- Players can experiment with genetic combinations to create unique agents.

#### 5. Education
- Education systems that allow agents to learn skills and gain knowledge over time.
- Different educational institutions will offer varying benefits.

#### 6. Culture
- Cultural influences on agent behavior, civilization progression, and interactions.
- Players can shape culture through choices in art, celebration, and community activities.

#### 7. Religion
- In-game religions can provide bonuses or penalties based on players' choices.
- Conflict and cooperation can arise from differing beliefs.

#### 8. Economy
- A complex economic system involving trade, resources, and currency.
- Players can trade resources or use economic policies to influence gameplay.

#### 9. Environmental Adaptation
- Agents must adapt to their environments for survival, influencing their behaviors and choices.

### New Mechanics

#### 10. Disease Systems
- Introduce diseases that can affect agent health and civilization functioning.
- Players must research medicines and create public health policies.

#### 11. Political Systems
- A political framework where players can elect leaders, create laws, and manage civic responsibilities.

#### 12. War Mechanics
- Comprehensive systems for declaring war, waging battles, and post-war recovery.
- Strategy elements for managing military resources and troops.

#### 13. Magic Systems
- Introduce magical elements that can influence gameplay through spells, rituals, or supernatural events.

#### 14. Climate Change
- Long-term environmental changes impacting gameplay, resource availability, and agent survival.

#### 15. Natural Disasters
- Implement disasters like earthquakes, floods, and storms that create challenges for players to overcome.

#### 16. Population Dynamics
- Population growth and decline mechanics based on various influences, including health, education, and environment.

#### 17. Supply Chains
- A logistical framework for managing resources and goods across civilization.
- Players will need to maintain efficient supply lines for strategic advantages.

#### 18. Art and Music
- Systems for creating and appreciating art and music within the game, influencing culture and morale.

#### 19. Technology Trees
- A branching technology tree allowing players to unlock advancements tailored to their strategy and gameplay style.

#### 20. Diplomacy
- Complex diplomatic relations that allow for alliances, treaties, and negotiations with other players and NPC factions.

#### 21. Espionage
- Mechanics for spying on other civilizations, gathering intelligence, and conducting covert operations.

#### 22. Detailed Survival Mechanics
- Introduce a more nuanced survival system that considers hunger, shelter, and agent morale.

### Conclusion
This expanded game design document encapsulates a wide array of gameplay mechanics, providing a foundation for creating an immersive and dynamic gaming experience in Genesis World. Further updates and expansions will be added as development progresses.
