# Agent Mechanics Documentation

## Overview
This document provides comprehensive details about the mechanics associated with agents within the Genesis World. Agents represent the autonomous entities that interact with the environment and other agents, driven by various systems, characteristics, and behaviors.

## 1. Agent Creation
Agents are instantiated through a specific process that includes defining their initial parameters, such as physical attributes, capabilities, and base personality traits. This includes:
- **Model Selection:** Different models lead to varying behaviors and capabilities. 
- **Initialization:** Assigning initial stats and properties to agents.

## 2. Spawning
Agents can be spawned into the world under various conditions. Spawning mechanics manage:
- **Location:** Determining where agents appear in the game world.
- **Timing:** Specifying spawn rates relative to game events and player actions.

## 3. Personality Archetypes
Each agent possesses a personality that influences their interactions and decisions. Common archetypes include:
- **Leader:** Charismatic and decisive agents.
- **Supporter:** Focused on assisting other agents.
- **Rebel:** Agents that challenge authority and norms.

## 4. Needs Systems
Agents have fundamental needs that must be managed, including:
- **Hunger:** Requires regular feeding to maintain functionality.
- **Thirst:** Necessitates access to water or liquid sustenance.
- **Social Interaction:** Needs for companionship and relationships with other agents.

## 5. Death Mechanics
Agents can experience death, which impacts the game state significantly. Key elements include:
- **Causes of Death:** Such as starvation, injury, or environmental factors.
- **Repercussions:** How death affects living agents and game progression.

## 6. Relationships
Agents form complex relationships with one another, characterized by:
- **Friendship:** Positive interactions fostering alliances.
- **Rivalry:** Competitive dynamics that may lead to conflict.
- **Family Ties:** Inherited relationships through reproduction.

## 7. Skills
Agents can develop skills over time, which are categorized as:
- **Combat Skills:** Proficiency in defense and offensive actions.
- **Crafting Skills:** Ability to create and manipulate items.
- **Social Skills:** Competence in forming and maintaining relationships.

## 8. Aging
Aging mechanics dictate how agents evolve through the lifespan. Factors include:
- **Stages of Life:** Young, Adult, and Elder stages with respective changes.
- **Physical and Mental Decline:** Addressing gradual loss of abilities.

## 9. Reproduction
Reproduction allows agents to create new entities, featuring:
- **Genetic Traits:** Passed down through generations.
- **Breeding Mechanics:** Conditions under which reproduction occurs.

## 10. Genetics
Genetic systems determine agent characteristics and potential, involving:
- **Dominant and Recessive Traits:** Influencing abilities and appearance.
- **Mutation:** Random changes that can lead to diversity and uniqueness.

## 11. Morale
Agent morale impacts their performance and decision-making. Elements include:
- **Motivation Levels:** Influenced by achievements, relationships, and overall well-being.
- **Consequences of Low Morale:** Potential to lead agents to negative actions or rebellion.

## 12. AI Decision Making
Agents utilize AI to make decisions based on:
- **Algorithms:** Evaluative measures for determining actions to state.
- **Behavior Trees:** Hierarchical systems guiding agent behaviors based on conditions.
