# Genetics and Evolution System Documentation

## DNA Traits
- **Definition**: DNA traits are biological attributes influenced by genetic information passed from one generation to another. Each trait can be categorized as dominant or recessive.
- **Categories**:
  - Physical Traits: Size, color, and shape characteristics.
  - Behavioral Traits: Instinctual behaviors and tendencies.
  - Physiological Traits: Metabolism, health, and survivability attributes.

## Generational Adaptation
- **Concept**: Over successive generations, species may adapt to their environment through a gradual process where advantageous traits become more common.
- **Mechanism**:
  - Environmental Pressures: Survival challenges that influence trait validity.
  - Natural Selection: Favoring the reproduction of individuals with beneficial traits.
  - Genetic Drift: Random changes in allele frequencies in a population.

## Mutations
- **Definition**: Random alterations in the DNA sequence that can lead to new traits.
- **Types**:
  - Point Mutations: Changes in a single nucleotide.
  - Insertions/Deletions: Addition or removal of DNA segments.
- **Effects**:
  - Beneficial Mutations: Provide an advantage for survival.
  - Neutral Mutations: Do not affect the organism's fitness.
  - Harmful Mutations: Detrimental to organism's survival chances.

## Breeding Mechanics
- **Process**: Breeding involves pairing two organisms to produce offspring.
- **Phenotypic Selection**: Breeders can choose which traits to prioritize in offspring.
- **Genotype Testing**: Allows prediction of potential traits based on parental genetics.

## Hereditary Systems
- **Inheritance Patterns**: Traits can be inherited in various manners:
  - Mendelian Inheritance: Traits are determined by a single gene.
  - Polygenic Inheritance: Traits influenced by multiple genes.
- **Genetic Recombination**: The process in which genes are shuffled during reproduction, leading to offspring with varying trait combinations.

## Summary
The genetics and evolution system provides a framework for understanding how traits are inherited, expressed, and how species adapt over time. By manipulating these factors, players can explore diverse evolutionary outcomes.
